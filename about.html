About me

Background

Ian Simpson was born in San Diego and grew up in North County San Diego (760 area code). His family is mostly secular in practice so he did not have a strong connection to any culture or belief growing up. Throughout schooltime he tried different pursuits, but none seemed to stick. This led to a sense of being lost and to a certain extent despair. Alongside failure in school, family issues, and a relatively weak body, he found this sense overwhelming. When looking for role models he saw society looks up to those that seem superficially happy and carefree(bums). So his pursuits took a further downturn. After he caused himself many problems, he finally got the idea that he was looking for purpose in the wrong place. Along the way, some talents were gifted to him. So, he saw life looking up, and investigated self-help, philosophy, and trades. However, it was still missing something and that something felt very significant. One day in community college philosophy class the professor introduced an idea that if the atheists are Heaven forbid right and everything is completely physical and secular, then there would still be no downside to following a religion and believing in something. However, if a religion is right and one does not follow it there would be a serious problem. Therefore, Ian started to investigate more seriously the far eastern religions that were popular in the San Diego region due to its "surfer/hippy" scene(bums). He found no strong connection to these to say the least and continued with an agnostic/self-help mindset. After a while he finished quite a few of his books and saw he had on the shelf a new international version bible that his grandmother sent him. So, he thought he will give it a try. He started in the first pages, as one does with other books, and found himself in an albeit badly translated and almost illegible version of the Holy Sefer Beresheet. He learned about Adam, Eve, Noah, Abraham, Isaac, Jacob, The Twelve Tribes, and many other significant figures. As he learned he felt an immense connection to the historical, geographical, and scientific proofs; realistic and powerful stories; and awesome beauty of the Holy Torah. Continuing through the Five Books of Moses, he encountered the mighty verses that could not be hampered by the poor translation and lack of commentary.

However, Ian didn't know what a Jew was or even what each religion believed in. After a course of roughly another year he investigated scriptural and philosophical sources of the three main religions in the world and came to the conclusion that only Orthodox Judaism contains the Truth. He discovered Rabbis Yosef Mizrahi, Yaron Reuven, and Yehoshua Zitron. Through their lectures based on scientific and logical proofs his choice was constantly reaffirmed. After about 2 more years he discovered Rabbi Lev Leib Lerner and continues to learn from these wonderful teachers. He hopes to move to a Haredi community and become Jewish himself. Each day is a blessing and we cannot thank HASHEM enough for the kindness, truth, and mercy that He has done with us.

Music

Music was one of Ian's first passions and he spent much time listening to a variety growing up. In high school, Ian learned basics of guitar, as well as electronic music with FL Studio. In Senior year, Ian released an album featuring his hip-hop inspired beats. As he discovered the Holy Torah, music has taken on a different role in His life. He now understands that music is a powerful vessel meant for the service of G-d and not for things that are contrary to that goal.
